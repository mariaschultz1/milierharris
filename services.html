01 What we do

Sound structures, important opportunities, fewer points of avoidable friction

Milier Harris helps clients establish sound structures, assess important opportunities, reduce avoidable friction, and maintain continuity across personal and financial matters.

We are comfortable in situations that are detailed, time-sensitive, or cross-jurisdictional. We are equally attentive to the administrative groundwork that keeps larger plans credible.

02 Capabilities

Specific frameworks for individual directives

Milier Harris provides multidisciplinary oversight for private capital. We architect specific frameworks tailored to individual client directives. By integrating specialised technical knowledge, we execute complex mandates with efficiency and structural integrity.

  • — 01

    Wealth and investment advisory

    We advise on portfolio positioning, risk concentration, liquidity, asset allocation, and manager oversight. We aim to deliver objective market analysis and construct resilient portfolios whilst prioritising capital preservation and targeted growth. Milier Harris advisors additionally evaluate risk parameters to mandate capital allocation effectively.

  • — 02

    Wealth and tax planning

    Planning should be technically sound and operationally workable. We coordinate wealth structures, reporting lines, ownership arrangements, and tax-sensitive decisions with a close eye on jurisdiction, timing, and family context. We coordinate legal protocols alongside financial strategies to optimise international positions. Our frameworks aim to mitigate exposure and ensure strict regulatory adherence.

  • — 03

    Real estate

    We advise on the acquisition, financing, holding, and oversight of global residential and commercial property. That may include support with structuring, lender dialogue, ownership vehicles, reporting, or the day-to-day organisation around a property portfolio.

  • — 04

    Family governance

    We design formal structures that organise communication and resolve disputes. Establishing clear protocols preserves institutional stability across generations. We help families put shape around ownership, communication, responsibility, and the practical operation of shared assets. That can include family charters, meeting structures, decision protocols, or the preparation of next-generation members for future roles.

  • — 05

    Corporate advisory

    Principals often hold significant private enterprise assets. We provide support around corporate structuring, board-related matters, major transactions, and business continuity questions, with the aim of enhancing operational value in the long-term.

  • — 06

    Lifestyle and concierge

    A well-run office should also reduce the operational burden on the client. We assist with personal administration, travel coordination, household support, security-related organisation, and selected day-to-day arrangements that require trusted oversight. This technical support allows Milier Harris clients to direct their energy toward their primary objectives.

  • — 07

    Art and collectibles

    Fine art and specialised assets demand distinct handling. We support clients with acquisition coordination, due diligence, insurance, storage considerations, logistics, and disposition planning.

  • — 08

    Succession planning

    Transferring authority and capital requires deliberate mechanics. Succession is a process that touches ownership, control, tax, governance, readiness, and family communication. Milier Harris helps clients prepare for that process in a structured way by establishing trusts and specialised legal instruments.

  • — 09

    Generational education

    Financial stewardship is a learned discipline. We build bespoke curriculums to prepare future stakeholders for wealth administration. Milier Harris works with families to introduce younger members to financial literacy, ownership responsibility, investment basics, governance principles, and the practical realities of inherited complexity.

  • — 10

    Relocation advisory

    A move between jurisdictions can affect residency, reporting, structures, assets, schools, staffing, and daily arrangements. We help clients prepare for those changes in a coordinated way, managing the functional realities of international transitions to ensure minimal disruption.

03 Success stories

Applied methodology and technical capabilities

The following scenarios demonstrate Milier Harris's applied methodology and technical capabilities.

— Scenario A

Multi-jurisdictional restructuring

The challenge. A principal sought to consolidate scattered international corporate entities ahead of a planned geographical relocation. The existing structures exposed the capital to overlapping tax liabilities and severe administrative friction.

The execution. Milier Harris orchestrated a rigorous audit of the client's global footprint. We reviewed entities across multiple jurisdictions and identified redundant structures. We coordinated with specialist local counsel to dissolve underperforming regional entities whilst simultaneously establishing a centralised holding company designed to manage cross-border governance and compliance requirements.

The resolution. The client achieved a significantly streamlined corporate structure, reducing unnecessary administrative expenditure and improving operational oversight. The restructuring also enhanced tax efficiency and simplified compliance obligations ahead of the client's formal change of residency.

— Scenario B

Generational transition of a primary asset

The challenge. An ageing founder required a legal and governance framework capable of transferring ownership of a privately held enterprise to the next generation without relinquishing immediate operational control.

The execution. We designed a bifurcated trust structure separating voting control from economic ownership. This enabled the founder to retain executive authority whilst progressively transferring beneficial interests to the next generation. Alongside the legal restructuring, Milier Harris implemented a phased governance education programme to prepare successors for future leadership and board responsibilities.

The resolution. Ownership was transferred successfully with no interruption to business continuity or executive decision-making. The structure reduced the risk of future governance disputes whilst establishing a clear succession pathway for long-term stewardship of the enterprise.

— Scenario C

Real estate portfolio consolidation

The challenge. A client maintained a fragmented portfolio of prime commercial property assets across multiple regulatory jurisdictions, resulting in elevated operating costs and highly disjointed financial reporting.

The execution. Our team led a full restructuring of the portfolio, consolidating all property holdings beneath a unified corporate structure. Milier Harris renegotiated existing debt facilities and standardised financial reporting procedures. This allowed us to introduce centralised asset and property management protocols to improve operational efficiency.

The resolution. The consolidation drastically reduced operational overhead and clarified the overarching risk profile. The client had significantly improved visibility across their portfolio, and unified reporting and operational oversight contributed to a measurable increase in net portfolio yield.

## 01 · The brand at a glance

> Milier Harris stewards the wealth of a small number of families across generations. The brand exists to signal three things without ever raising its voice: permanence, discretion, and judgement.

**In one line**

A quiet, modern Swiss identity for a multi-family office — confident enough to withhold.

**Principles**

- **Discretion first** — We never advertise wealth; we hold it. The brand is understated by design.
- **Permanence** — Built for the next generation, not the next quarter. Nothing trend-driven.
- **Discipline** — Clarity, whitespace and restraint, in the lineage of Swiss design.
- **Continuity** — Two names woven into one structure, mirrored in the interlocking mark.

## 02 · Concept & story

### The idea

The Milier Harris monogram interlocks an M and an H — two letters woven from a single system of parallel strokes, sharing one structure rather than standing side by side. That interlock is the whole story of a multi-family office: distinct families, distinct names, integrated into one disciplined framework that endures across generations.

### How it should feel

Like the reception of a private Geneva institution rather than a bank atrium. Calm, exact, and quietly certain. The brand earns trust by what it leaves out — no gloss, no spectacle, no noise.

### What we avoid

The visual clichés of the wealth industry: skylines, yachts, gradients, globes, chess pieces, hands cradling abstract orbs. Old wealth reads these as new money trying too hard. Our restraint is the differentiator.

## 04 · Clear space & sizing

Give the mark room. Clear space on all sides equals one stroke unit (x) — the width of a single bar in the monogram. Nothing (type, image edges, other logos) may enter this zone.

![Clear space construction](clearspace.png)

**Minimum sizes**

- **Standalone mark** — Monogram: 12 mm tall in print, 40 px on screen.
- **Primary lockup** — Full lockup: 28 mm tall in print, 150 px on screen.
- **Why** — Below these, legibility of the interlock and wordmark breaks down.

## 05 · Logo misuse

The mark's integrity depends on consistency.

**Never**

- ✕ Recolour the monogram in anything but ink, paper, or — exceptionally — Léman.
- ✕ Stretch, condense, rotate or skew the mark.
- ✕ Add effects: shadows, glows, gradients, bevels, outlines.
- ✕ Reconstruct or alter the stroke spacing of the monogram.
- ✕ Place the mark on a busy photograph or low-contrast ground.
- ✕ Box the logo or set it at an angle.

**Always**

- ✓ Use the supplied master files at the correct proportions.
- ✓ Preserve clear space and minimum sizes.
- ✓ Favour ink-on-paper; reserve the dark lockup for dark grounds.

## 06 · Colour

The palette is deliberately narrow: warm paper, near-black ink, and a single accent — a deep lake blue-green drawn from Lac Léman. One hue, two depths. Restraint is the point.

![Colour swatches](swatches.png)

| Name | Hex | RGB | Role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Ink** | `#1A1A18` | 26 · 26 · 24 | Primary. Type, mark, all structure. |
| **Paper** | `#F4F1EA` | 244 · 241 · 234 | Primary background. Never pure white. |
| **Léman** | `#2E6E6A` | 46 · 110 · 106 | Sparing. Links, rules, one detail per view. |
| **Deep** | `#173B3A` | 23 · 59 · 58 | Large fills, footers, when accent must calm. |
| **Stone** | `#6B6A64` | 107 · 106 · 100 | Secondary text, captions, dividers, UI hints. |

### Using the accent

Léman is a guest, not a host. As a rule, no more than one accented element per view — a rule, a link, a single number, a small detail. If a layout needs two greens to feel balanced, it has too much green. Ink and paper carry the work; the lake colour simply marks the one thing that matters most.

Never use pure black (`#000000`) or pure white (`#FFFFFF`). Ink and Paper are warmer and read as considered rather than default.

## 07 · Typography

Two voices. A clean grotesque sans does almost all the work — it echoes the geometry of the mark and keeps the system contemporary and Swiss. A high-contrast editorial serif appears only for occasional display moments, to add a note of warmth without softening the discipline.

### Recommended faces

- **Sans (primary)** — Söhne, Neue Haas Grotesk, or Inter for the functional layer.
- **Serif (accent)** — Canela, Tiempos Headline, or a Didone for display only.

These are recommendations; any well-drawn neo-grotesque plus a single high-contrast serif will hold the system together.

### Hierarchy

| Role | Typeface / weight | Size | Tracking | Case |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Display / H1 | Editorial serif · Regular | 40–60 pt | 0 | Sentence |
| Headings / H2 | Primary sans · Medium | 22–28 pt | 0 | Sentence |
| Body | Primary sans · Regular | 10–11 pt | 0 | Sentence |
| Eyebrow / label | Primary sans · Regular | 8–9 pt | +6% | UPPER |
| Caption | Primary sans · Light | 8 pt | 0 | Sentence |

Set body text generously: long measure, ample leading, sentence case throughout. We never set headlines in all-caps; the only capitalised text is the small tracked eyebrow label.

## 08 · The line motif

The monogram is built from parallel strokes of equal weight. That rhythm extends into a system device: a set of fine, evenly-spaced lines that can frame a page edge, divide sections, sit behind a hero, or watermark a document — tying every surface back to the mark without ever repeating the logo.

![Line motif](motif.png)

**How to use it**

- **Placement** — Edge of a website hero, the foot of a letterhead, a section break.
- **Rhythm** — Equal weight, even gaps. One line may take the Léman accent — never more.
- **Restraint** — Always quiet. The motif suggests structure; it never becomes decoration.

## 09 · Voice & tone

We write the way we design: plainly, precisely, and without performance. We are advisors, not salespeople. The reader should feel informed and respected, never sold to.

**We are**

- **Clear** — Short sentences. Concrete nouns. No jargon, no superlatives.
- **Measured** — We make considered statements and stand behind them.
- **Discreet** — Warm but never familiar; professional but never cold.

**We are not**

- **Boastful** — No "bespoke synergies," no "unparalleled solutions," no hype.
- **Indiscreet** — We never reference clients' wealth, names or holdings.

> "We look after a small number of families, and we intend to do so for a long time."

An example of the register: a plain claim, quietly made.

## 10 · Applications

How the system behaves across the touchpoints a family actually encounters.

- **Stationery** — Stacked or full lockup, deep margins, a single hairline. Engraved or blind-embossed monogram where budget allows.
- **Website** — Paper ground, generous whitespace, serif display headline, the line motif at the hero edge. Léman reserved for links and one accent per screen.
- **Reports & decks** — Ink covers, paper interiors, large quiet numerals, plenty of air. Never busy.
- **Avatar / favicon** — Monogram only, centred, on paper or ink. No wordmark at small sizes.

## 11 · Quick reference

**Colour** — Ink `#1A1A18` · Paper `#F4F1EA` · Léman `#2E6E6A` · Deep `#173B3A` · Stone `#6B6A64`. Never pure black or white. One accent per view.

**Type** — Grotesque sans for everything; high-contrast serif for display only. Sentence case. Caps only for the tracked eyebrow label.

**Logo** — Interlocking MH monogram leads. Clear space = one stroke unit. Min 12 mm / 40 px. Ink on paper by default.

**Spirit** — Permanence, discretion, judgement. When in doubt, remove something.
